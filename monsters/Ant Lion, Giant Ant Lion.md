# Ant Lion, Giant Ant Lion
This gigantic burrowing insect has an oversized abdomen. A terrifying set of long, hooked mandibles protrudes from its head.
**Source** Bestiary 3 pg. 17, Bonus Bestiary pg. 5
**XP** 1,600

N Large vermin
**Init** +0; **Senses** _[[spells/Darkvision|darkvision]]_ 60 ft., _[[universal monster rules/Tremorsense|tremorsense]]_ 60 ft.; Perception +0

##### Defense

**AC** 19, touch 9, _[[conditions/Flat-Footed|flat-footed]]_ 19 (+10 natural, –1 size)
**hp** 60 (8d8+24)
**Fort** +9, **Ref** +2, **Will** +2
**Immune** mind-affecting effects

##### Offense
**Speed** 30 ft., _[[universal monster rules/Burrow|burrow]]_ 10 ft.
**Melee** bite +10 (2d8+7 plus _[[universal monster rules/Grab|grab]]_)
**Space** 10 ft., **Reach** 5 ft.
**Special Attacks** sand trap

##### Statistics
**Str** 20, **Dex** 11, **Con** 17, **Int** —, **Wis** 11, **Cha** 10
**Base Atk** +6; **CMB** +12 (+16 grapple); **CMD** 22 (30 vs. _[[universal monster rules/Trip|trip]]_)

##### Ecology

**Environment** warm deserts
**Organization** solitary or nest (2–4)
**Treasure** incidental

### Special Abilities
**Sand Trap (Ex)** A giant ant _[[monsters/Lion|lion]]_ can create a 60-foot-diameter, 20-foot-deep pit in any sand or soft earth surface. Creating a sand trap takes 1 hour. A DC 15 Perception check allows a creature to realize such a depression in the sand is in fact a trap. Any creature that steps into the trap slides to the center if it fails a DC 14 Reflex save—such victims take no damage, but they do fall _[[conditions/Prone|prone]]_. A giant ant _lion_ can make an attack of opportunity against any creature that falls to the bottom of its sand trap. These creatures can move across sand traps at their normal speed and are immune to the trap’s effects. Other creatures can navigate the trap’s walls with a DC 20 _[[universal monster rules/Climb|Climb]]_ check.

##### Description

Perils of the desert depths, giant ant lions construct traps from the shifting sands. These beasts lurk at the base of these pits, half buried and patiently awaiting unwary prey.
